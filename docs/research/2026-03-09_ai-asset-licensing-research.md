# AI-Generated Asset Licensing for Public-Facing Projects

**Purpose:** Document licensing terms of AI image generation tools to inform asset selection for public/commercial use.
**Target Outcome:** Licensing checklist and tool comparison for content operations.

## Background

During S4, we discovered that the Take AI Bite logo (generated with Recraft AI free tier) could not be used commercially. This triggered a full replacement. This research documents the findings for future reference.

## Findings

### Recraft AI

- **Free tier:** No commercial use rights. Images remain Recraft's property. Policy effective since 2024-08-12.
- **Free tier visibility:** Images appear in public community gallery. Deleted images not permanently removed.
- **Paid tier:** Full ownership and commercial rights at generation time.
- **Source:** https://www.recraft.ai/blog/ownership-and-commercial-use-faq

### Ideogram

- **Free tier:** No ownership claim by Ideogram. No limits on commercial use.
- **Free tier visibility:** Images appear in public gallery.
- **Quality:** High quality for logo/vector work.
- **Source:** https://ideogram.ai/

### Leonardo.ai

- **Free tier:** Non-exclusive, royalty-free commercial rights. 150 fast tokens/day.
- **Free tier visibility:** Creations appear in public feed where others may remix.
- **Source:** https://leonardo.ai/

### DeepAI

- **Free tier:** 100% rights retained by creator.
- **Quality:** Lower quality for logo/design work compared to Ideogram and Leonardo.
- **Source:** https://deepai.org/

### General Legal Context

- AI-generated images have uncertain copyright status in many jurisdictions
- US Copyright Office has ruled that purely AI-generated images without human authorship are not copyrightable (2023)
- Platform ToS grants (commercial license) are separate from copyright ownership
- Mechanical transformations (flip, recolor, pixelate) of a copyrighted work do not create a new derivative work

## Recommendations

1. **Before using any AI-generated asset publicly:** check the tool's ToS for the specific tier being used
2. **Prefer tools that explicitly grant commercial rights on free tier:** Ideogram, Leonardo.ai, DeepAI
3. **Document licensing alongside the asset:** tool name, tier, date, licensing status
4. **Be aware of public gallery clauses:** most free tiers expose your images publicly
5. **Keep the original source file:** store in `docs/assets/` alongside derived files
